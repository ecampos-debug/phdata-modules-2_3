⚡ The 5-Part Prompt Recipe — use this every time

1
Role
Tell AI who to act as
"Act as an experienced recruiter…"
2
Task
Tell AI exactly what you want
"Draft a LinkedIn InMail…"
3
Context
Give relevant background
"The role is Sr. DE, hybrid, Minneapolis…"
4
Constraints
Set limits & guardrails
"Under 150 words, no jargon…"
5
Output
Specify the format
"Write as a short paragraph, ready to send…"

Quick Tips — the 60-second version

✅Vague prompt = vague answer. Be specific.
✅Always tell it the audience (HM, candidate, exec)
✅Specify tone: professional / casual / concise
✅If the first output isn't right — refine, don't restart
🚫Never paste candidate names or personal data
🚫Never send AI output without reviewing it first

Boolean String Builder
Best for: LinkedIn Recruiter, job boards
When to use
You need a Boolean search string for a role and want to save 10+ minutes of manual string-building.
Act as an expert technical recruiter with 10+ years sourcing data and AI talent. Build a Boolean search string for LinkedIn Recruiter to find candidates for a [JOB TITLE] role at a [COMPANY TYPE]. Key requirements: - [SKILL 1] - [SKILL 2] - [SKILL 3] Location: [CITY / REMOTE] Exclude: [TITLES OR COMPANIES TO EXCLUDE] Output a clean Boolean string I can paste directly into LinkedIn Recruiter. Then give me 2 alternate versions — one broader, one narrower.

Two Levels, One JD
Best for: Lead / Principal, Sr / Lead roles
When to use
You need one posting that covers two levels without anchoring candidate expectations to a specific title.
Act as an organizational psychologist and executive recruiting expert with 20+ years in consulting. I need to write a single job description that covers both [LEVEL 1, e.g. Lead] and [LEVEL 2, e.g. Principal] for a [ROLE NAME] at phData. The posting title should read: [BROAD TITLE, e.g. "Data Governance Leader"] At the top of the JD body, add: "Target Levels: Lead or Principal" In the responsibilities and requirements sections, include clear differentiators for each level so candidates can self-identify — without us committing to a title upfront. Here is the draft JD to work from: [PASTE JD HERE] Condense it, remove redundancies, keep all critical information, and add level differentiators. Ask me if you need clarification.

JD Simplifier & Tightener
Best for: cleaning up long or stale JDs
When to use
A JD is too long, has jargon, or doesn't reflect what we're actually hiring for.
Act as a senior talent brand specialist at a data and AI consulting firm. Rewrite the job description below to be: - Shorter (target: under 400 words) - Clear and jargon-free - Compelling to a [SENIORITY LEVEL] candidate who values impact and technical challenge - Accurate to what we actually need (don't add fluff) Keep all must-have requirements. Remove anything that's nice-to-have padding. JD to rewrite: [PASTE JD HERE]

Messaging
LinkedIn InMail — First Touch
Best for: outbound passive candidate outreach
When to use
You're reaching out cold to a passive candidate and want a message that gets a reply.
Act as an experienced recruiter at a top-tier data and AI consulting firm. Write a first-touch LinkedIn InMail to a passive candidate for a [JOB TITLE] role. Candidate background (from their profile): - Current role: [CURRENT TITLE & COMPANY] - Key experience: [1-2 RELEVANT THINGS FROM THEIR PROFILE] About the role: - [1-2 COMPELLING THINGS ABOUT THE ROLE OR COMPANY] Constraints: - Under 100 words - Conversational, not salesy - End with a low-friction question (not "are you open to new opportunities?") - Do not mention compensation

Candidate Follow-Up Message
Best for: after interview, no response, offer stage
When to use
You need to follow up with a candidate at any stage and want it to feel human, not templated.
Act as a thoughtful, candidate-focused recruiter. Write a follow-up message to a candidate after [STAGE: e.g., recruiter screen / final interview / offer sent]. Context: - Candidate name: [FIRST NAME ONLY — remove before sending to AI] - What happened: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF LAST INTERACTION] - Next step: [WHAT YOU NEED FROM THEM OR WHAT'S HAPPENING NEXT] Tone: warm, direct, respectful of their time Length: 3–5 sentences max Format: ready to send via email or LinkedIn

Summarizing
Recruiter Screen Summary
Best for: HM-ready candidate summaries
When to use
Right after a recruiter screen. Paste your raw notes and get a clean, structured summary for the hiring manager.
Act as a senior recruiter summarizing a candidate screen for a hiring manager. Role: [JOB TITLE] Here are my raw notes from the screen: [PASTE YOUR NOTES — no candidate full name or personal data] Produce a structured summary with these four sections: 1. Snapshot (2–3 sentences: who they are, overall impression) 2. Preferences (what they're looking for, motivations, flexibility) 3. Highlights (top 3 strengths relevant to this role) 4. Flags (anything to probe deeper or that's a concern) Keep it concise. Write it for a hiring manager who has 60 seconds to read it.

Intake Meeting Summary
Best for: post-intake recap to HM or TA ops
When to use
After an intake call — turn messy notes into a clean, shareable summary that aligns everyone on the req.
Act as a recruiting coordinator summarizing an intake meeting. Role: [JOB TITLE] Hiring Manager: [HM FIRST NAME / TEAM] Here are my intake notes: [PASTE NOTES] Summarize into: - Role overview (1–2 sentences) - Must-have requirements (bullet list) - Nice-to-have requirements (bullet list) - Red flags / dealbreakers - Interview process agreed on - Any open questions to follow up on Keep it scannable. Write it so a recruiter who wasn't on the call can run with it.

Screening
Custom Screening Questions
Best for: application screeners, phone screens
When to use
You need a set of screening questions that will quickly surface whether a candidate is worth advancing.
Act as a senior technical recruiter at a data and AI consulting firm. Generate 5 screening questions for a [JOB TITLE] role. Requirements to screen for: - [REQUIREMENT 1] - [REQUIREMENT 2] - [REQUIREMENT 3] For each question, include: - The question itself - What a strong answer looks like (1 sentence) - What's a red flag answer (1 sentence) Keep questions open-ended. Avoid yes/no questions. Focus on real experience, not hypotheticals.

Candidate Rejection Email
Best for: post-screen, post-interview declines
When to use
You need to decline a candidate respectfully — at any stage — without it sounding like a form letter.
Act as a recruiter who cares about candidate experience. Write a rejection email for a candidate who reached [STAGE: e.g., recruiter screen / final interview] for a [JOB TITLE] role. Reason for decline (internal only — don't state this directly): [REASON] Tone: warm, honest, respectful — not corporate Length: 4–6 sentences Do: thank them genuinely, leave the door open if appropriate Do not: use phrases like "we've decided to move forward with other candidates" or "we'll keep your resume on file" No candidate name needed — I'll personalize before sending.
