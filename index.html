The 5-Part Prompt Recipe
▼
Screen 2 · Concept

Think of AI as a New Recruiter

If you say to a new recruiter, "Can you help with this candidate?" — you'll get something generic. The quality of the output depends on how clear your instructions are.

AI works the same way: better prompts = better results.

We use a simple 5-part recipe for every prompt:

Screen 3 · The Recipe

The 5 Parts of a Strong Prompt

Part 1
🎭 Role
"Act as an experienced technical recruiter at a data & AI consulting firm."
Part 2
📋 Task
"Write a first-touch LinkedIn InMail to a passive candidate."
Part 3
📌 Context
"Senior Data Engineer role, remote, Snowflake + dbt, hiring manager skims."
Part 4
🚧 Constraints
"Under 120 words. Conversational, not salesy. Keep it job-related."
Part 5
📄 Output
"3 short paragraphs: hook, value, soft CTA." or "4 labeled sections: Snapshot, Preferences, Highlights, Flags."
Task vs. Output — don't overthink it These two will sometimes feel similar, and that's okay. Task tells AI what to do; Output tells it what to hand back. When in doubt, make sure your Output instruction specifies format, length, and structure — that's the part most people skip.
Guardrail Reminder Even with a great prompt: avoid PII in unapproved tools · keep prompts job-related and neutral · review every output before sharing.

Prompt Examples
▼
Screen 4 · Example 1

Example – Outreach Prompt

Here's a full 5-part prompt for a first-touch LinkedIn outreach:

RoleAct as an experienced recruiter at a top-tier data & AI consulting firm.
TaskWrite a first-touch LinkedIn InMail to a passive candidate for a Senior Data Engineer role.
ContextCandidate: currently a Data Engineer at a mid-size fintech. Experience with Snowflake and dbt. Role: fully remote, focused on building data pipelines for enterprise clients.
ConstraintsUnder 100 words. Conversational, not salesy. End with a low-friction question. Don't mention compensation or relocation.
OutputWrite as a short message, ready to send in LinkedIn — no placeholders.
Focus on the structure, not memorization The exact wording will vary by role and candidate. What stays constant is the 5-part structure.
Screen 5 · Example 2

Example – Recruiter Screen Summary Prompt

Here's a prompt to turn messy screen notes into a hiring-manager-ready summary:

RoleAct as a recruiter summarizing a candidate screen for a busy hiring manager.
TaskTurn my raw notes into a structured hiring manager summary.
ContextRole: Senior Data Engineer. Candidate is currently at [Company]. Key skills: [skills]. I'll paste my notes below.
ConstraintsKeep it neutral and job-related. No assumptions about personality or motivation. Under 250 words.
OutputProduce 4 labeled sections: 1) Snapshot (2–3 sentences) 2) Preferences 3) Highlights (top 3 strengths) 4) Flags.
Connection to TA Agents This pattern is similar to how our Recruiter Screen Summarizer agent works. Better prompts → better agent behavior.

TA AI Tool Map (Overview)

Screen 2 · Big Picture

The TA AI Tool Ecosystem

Think of our AI tools as an ecosystem, not a pile of apps. Each tool has a job:

  • Glean — our enterprise AI layer that sits on top of Drive, Gmail, Slack, Confluence, and more. Powers our TA agents.
  • BrightHire — AI-assisted interview platform for recording, transcription, and structured summaries.

Deep Dive – Glean & BrightHire
▼
Screen 4 · Glean

Glean – Our Enterprise AI & Agent Platform

What it is: Glean is our enterprise AI layer. It connects to approved systems (Google Drive, Gmail, Slack, Confluence, Jira, etc.) and respects permissions — you only see what you're already allowed to see.

Why it matters for TA:

  • Ask questions like: "Show me recent Advisory recruiter playbooks" or "Summarize this candidate's past interviews and scorecards" (via agents).
  • Run TA agents built on Glean: Recruiter Screen Summarizer, Prompt Optimizer, Boolean Optimizer, JD Agent, Resume Screener, Scorecard helpers, and more.
  • Data access rules already set in your systems apply — if you can't see a doc in Drive, you won't see it in Glean.
Key TakeawayUse Glean when you need to work with internal information or run TA-specific agents.
Screen 5 · BrightHire

BrightHire – Interview Recording & Summaries

What it is: BrightHire is our interview intelligence platform. It records authorized interviews, generates transcripts, and produces AI-generated summaries aligned to our scorecards.

Why it matters for TA:

  • It's the only approved tool for recording interviews.
  • It gives us a reliable record of what was said — supporting quality and fairness.
  • AI templates can summarize interviews in sections aligned to our scorecards.

How it connects: BrightHire integrates with Greenhouse for scheduling and tagging. Summaries and notes inform scorecards and follow-up discussions.

Key TakeawayUse BrightHire for anything related to live interviews and intake calls — recording, transcription, and structured notes.

Deep Dive – ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini & Copilot
▼
Screen 6 · When to Use ChatGPT & Claude

ChatGPT & Claude – For Outputs Glean Can't Build

The rule of thumb: Start with Glean. Use ChatGPT or Claude only when Glean can't produce what you need.

Concrete examples of when ChatGPT / Claude make sense:

  • Building a formatted HTML artifact or slide deck (e.g., this Learning Academy module was built in Claude).
  • Creating a net-new JD template from scratch — no internal doc to reference, starting from a blank page.
  • Writing a new scorecard or interview guide structure when no existing template fits the role.
  • Drafting a new prompt for the Prompt Library when you want to test and refine it before adding it to Glean.
  • Building something with complex formatting (tables, multi-section layouts) that Glean doesn't render well.

Important guardrails:

  • Use only approved company accounts — not personal accounts.
  • Do not paste candidate names, contact info, or confidential client details.
  • Treat all outputs as drafts — review before using or sharing.
Key TakeawayIf Glean can do it, use Glean. ChatGPT / Claude are for specific outputs that require formatting, creation from scratch, or capabilities outside Glean's scope.
